Paying Limits let you define a budget envelope that Archer enforces every time a payout is attempted. These limits are not soft application-layer checks — they are written to the ArcherAgentEndpoint smart contract and evaluated on-chain before each transfer executes. A transaction that exceeds any limit is reverted; no funds move.

## The three limit dimensions

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Max per payment" icon="circle-dollar-sign">
    The highest USD amount that can be sent in a single payout transaction. Maps to `maxAmountPerTx` in the contract.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Max per period" icon="chart-bar">
    The total USD that can be paid out across all transactions within the configured period. Maps to `maxAmountPerPeriod` in the contract.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Max transactions per period" icon="arrows-left-right">
    The number of payout calls allowed within the configured period. Maps to `maxCallsPerPeriod` in the contract.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Configuring a period

All three limits share a single time window. You define this window by entering a number and selecting a unit:

| Unit | Duration |
|---|---|
| Hours | 3,600 seconds per unit |
| Days | 86,400 seconds per unit |
| Weeks | 604,800 seconds per unit |
| Months | 2,592,000 seconds per unit |

For example, setting the period to `2 Weeks` means the per-period limits reset every two weeks, starting from the moment the first payout in that window executes.

## How enforcement works

When a payout is triggered, the ArcherAgentEndpoint contract checks all three limits before transferring any funds:

1. Is the payout amount within `maxAmountPerTx`?
2. Would this payout push the period total above `maxAmountPerPeriod`?
3. Would this payout exceed `maxCallsPerPeriod` for the current window?

If any check fails, the entire transaction reverts with a specific error (`MaxAmountPerTxExceeded`, `MaxAmountPerPeriodExceeded`, or `MaxCallsPerPeriodExceeded`). Nothing is deducted and the payout is not retried automatically.

<Warning>
Saving your Paying Limits submits a blockchain transaction from your smart wallet. Allow a few seconds for the transaction to confirm on Base before expecting the new limits to take effect. If you close the page before confirmation, your changes may not be saved.
</Warning>
